You will find some very useful tips for your vacations below

Paris:The city of lights

  • Skip the Long Lines – Buy skip-the-line tickets for the Eiffel Tower, Louvre, and Versailles to save hours of waiting.

  • Best Views of the Eiffel Tower – Avoid the crowded Trocadéro; instead, head to Rue de l’Université or the rooftop of Galeries Lafayette for stunning (and free!) views.

  • Use Public Transport – The Paris Metro is the fastest way to get around. Buy a Navigo Easy Pass for unlimited rides.

  • Eat Like a Local – Avoid restaurants near major landmarks; instead, dine in Le Marais or Saint-Germain-des-Prés for authentic and affordable French cuisine.

  • Free Museum Days – Many museums, including the Louvre, offer free entry on the first Sunday of the month (except in peak summer).

Rome:The eternal city

  • Early Bird Gets the Best Experience – Visit the Colosseum, Vatican, and Trevi Fountain early in the morning to avoid the crowds.

  • Dress Comfortably – Rome’s cobblestone streets are tough on your feet; wear comfortable walking shoes.

  • Use Public Fountains – Bring a refillable bottle and drink from the free nasoni fountains—Rome’s water is clean and delicious.

  • Order Like a Local – Cappuccino is only for breakfast! In the afternoon, switch to an espresso or caffè macchiato.

  • Watch Out for Tourist Traps – Restaurants with waiters outside trying to lure you in usually serve overpriced, low-quality food. Look for places filled with locals instead.


Dubai:The City of Luxury & Adventure

  • Best Time to Visit – Avoid the extreme summer heat! November to March is the best time for outdoor activities.

  • Affordable Transport – Taxis are expensive; use the Dubai Metro for fast and budget-friendly travel.

  • Dress Modestly in Public Areas – While Dubai is modern, it’s best to cover shoulders and knees in non-tourist zones.

  • Book Desert Safaris in Advance – Don’t wait until the last minute! Booking early gets you better deals on camel rides, dune bashing, and sandboarding.

  • Enjoy Free & Cheap Experiences – Watch the Dubai Fountain Show, visit Al Fahidi Historic District, or stroll through the Souks for an authentic Arabian experience

New York City: The City That Never Sleeps

  • Subway Over Taxis – Traffic is crazy; the NYC Subway is the fastest way to get around. Get a MetroCard for unlimited rides.

  • Broadway on a Budget – Use TKTS booths or apps like TodayTix to score discounted last-minute show tickets.

  • Walk the Brooklyn Bridge Early – Go at sunrise to avoid the crowds and enjoy breathtaking views.

  • Street Food is a Must – Skip the overpriced restaurants and try food trucks for delicious, local eats.

  • Free Attractions – Take the Staten Island Ferry for free Statue of Liberty views, or explore Central Park, Times Square, and The High Line at no cost.

Zanzibar: The island of spice and sunsets

  • Skip the Long Lines – Purchase tickets in advance for popular attractions like Stone Town, Spice Farms, and Jozani Forest to avoid waiting in long queues.

  • Best Views in Zanzibar – Head to the rooftop bars at The Swahili House or the rooftop of the Zanzibar Serena Hotel for stunning sunset views of the Indian Ocean.

  • Use Public Transport – The dala-dala (minibus) is a cheap and local way to get around Zanzibar. Alternatively, taxis or tuk-tuks are readily available for a more comfortable ride.

  • Eat Like a Local – For authentic Zanzibari cuisine, try the Forodhani Gardens Night Market in Stone Town, where you can enjoy fresh seafood and Swahili street food.

  • Explore the Spice Farms – Take a guided tour of one of Zanzibar's spice farms, where you can learn about and sample the island’s famous spices like cloves,etc



Thailand: The land of smiles and golden temples

  • Skip the Long Lines – Purchase skip-the-line tickets for popular attractions like the Grand Palace and Wat Pho to avoid wasting time in long queues.

  • Best Views in Thailand – For breathtaking views of Bangkok, head to the rooftop bars at Sky Bar or Vertigo, where you can enjoy panoramic cityscapes.

  • Use Public Transport – The Skytrain (BTS) and the MRT subway are the fastest and most convenient ways to get around Bangkok. Consider buying a Rabbit Card for easy access to both.

  • Eat Like a Local – Visit local markets such as Chatuchak or street food stalls around Sukhumvit for authentic Thai dishes like Pad Thai, Som Tum, and Mango Sticky Rice.

  • Free Temple Visits – Many temples, including Wat Arun, are free to enter during certain hours. Be sure to check their visiting times to avoid crowds.
